ABOUT US

A Christian Classical Curriculum built on the foundation of God's Word and written by well-known, seasoned classical educators and leaders.

The Rafiki Classical Christian Curriculum is a full-orbed, liberal arts educational system. From Pre K - 6th grade, our curriculum intergrates key academic subjects focusing on the great books of Wsetern civilization viewed through the lens of chronological history and built of the foundation of God's Word.

We bagan writing the Rafiki Christian Classical Curriculum over 18years ago for Rafiki schools in Africa. Our vision was to educate our students as image bearers of God, fostering their minds and affections to consider what is true, good and beautiful.

To accomplish this, we partnered with highly trained and devoted classical educators and scholars to craft a robust curriculum which equips students to be godly contributors who help their society.




Our History

Rosemary McEachern Jensen, born July 20, 1929, is the fourth of six children born to Don and Mayhoward McEachern. Raised in a log house on the banksof the St. Johns River near Jacksonville, Florida, Rosemary knew at an early age that she wnated to tell others about God. In the summer of 1946 when a pastor challenged a group of youth to give their lives to the foreign mission field, Rosemary felt compelled to step forward and answer the call. That one step began a seven-decade journey in God's service, first as amissionary to Tanzania, then as the executive director of the Bible Study Fellowship International, and most recently as the president of The Rafiki Foundation Inc.

The years from 1987 to 1991 saw the expansion of Rafiki's work into Kenya, Uganda, Nigeria, and Ghana. An increasing number of doctors, dentists, teachers, and business people answered God's call to bring medical care, viocational assistance, and Bible teaching to the African people. Funding for these projects was borne by private donors and the biblical studies were provided by Bible Study Fellowship. By 1992, however, the African continent was overwhelmed with the needs of millions of orphans due to the needs of millions of orphans due to the AIDS epidemic. In response to pleas from government and church officials to help the children of Africa, Gosemary expanded the role of the Foundation form that of a sending organization to one that provided for the needs of orphans and vulnerable children. Since then, the Foundation has established Rafiki Training Villages in ten of the poorest countries in sub-Saharan Africa to help meet that need.

The Foundation has again responded to requests foe hrlp from the African church and government leaders by providing eduactional curriculum for their schools. In 2009, Rafiki began to publish and distribute its school curriculum (including the Rafiki Bible Study) to African church schools. In countries where educational materials are in short supply, the curriculum is a tremendous gift and is another tangible way that Rfaiki is br=efriending the children of Africa.

In 2015 the capstone piece of Rafiki's education come to fruition. The Rafiki Institute of Classical Education (RICE) is a teachers college founded to eqip the next generation of classical educators. These colleges train students in Math, Science, History, Philosophy, Theology, Peadology, and more. Graduates are integrated into local and church schools, supplied with Rafiki curriculum, and ultimately set to bring classical education to schools across Africa.
